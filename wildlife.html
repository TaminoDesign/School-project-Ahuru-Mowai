The wildlife at
Āhuru Mōwai

Experience the fauna of Aotearoa

Wandering the boardwalks will take you by our small lake.
You will also come to the Team Tuatara pen where
you may spot, if you look carefully, juvenile tuatara
basking in the sun. Bird-feeding stations are dotted
alongside the boardwalk. Look for kākāriki, tui,
korimako (bellbird), hihi (stitchbird), tititi pounamu (rifleman),
pīwakawaka (fantail). Don't forget to check the ground.
Takahē are wandering and if you look closely, you may see
prod marks in the ground from kiwi beaks.

Petroica australis

Robin

At Risk

South Island robin adult males have a dark grey-black head and upper body, brownish-black feathers and tail and a white to yellowish-white lower breast and belly; adult females have a light to dark grey upper body and a smaller, less distinctly breast-belly area.

Abel Tasman Native bush right down to the sea, granite rocks of all different shapes, seals, birds and amazing golden sand beaches are all part of the Abel Tasman Track. The Abel Tasman Coast Track will take you through beautiful beech forests and other beautiful landscapes. Anchorage Bay is generally the first stop for adventurers. Side trips include Cascade Falls, Cleopatra's Pool, Tinline Bay.

Tongariro The Tongariro Alpine Crossing is a stunning 19.4km walk that takes adventurers into the mountains of the Central Plateau of the North Island. This great walk winds through glacial valleys, past ancient lava flows and stunning crater lakes. The mountains have cultural and spiritual significance to Ngāti Tuwharetoa, the local iwi.
